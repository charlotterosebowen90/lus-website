Understanding Your PSA Result — What the Numbers Mean

A raised PSA can cause significant anxiety, but it does not necessarily mean cancer. PSA levels can be elevated by many common conditions including an enlarged prostate, infection, and even vigorous exercise. Our dedicated PSA Testing page explains what your result means, what happens next, and why the modern MRI-first pathway means most men no longer need an immediate biopsy.

Kidney Stone Prevention — Simple Changes That Make a Difference

Up to 50% of patients who form a kidney stone will experience another within five years — but recurrence is not inevitable. Drinking at least 2.5 litres of fluid daily, reducing salt intake, maintaining normal calcium consumption (not restricting it), and moderating animal protein can significantly reduce your risk. Our Recurrent Stone Formers page covers metabolic investigation and personalised prevention in detail.

What to Expect at Your First Urology Appointment

If you have been referred to a urologist or are booking your first appointment, it is natural to feel unsure about what will happen. In most cases, your first visit will involve a detailed discussion of your symptoms and medical history, a clinical examination, and simple initial tests such as a urine dipstick and flow rate measurement. Our Patient Information page explains the process step by step, including what to bring, insurance information, and how to prepare.
