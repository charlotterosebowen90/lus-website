About

Leading Robotic Urological Surgeon

Mr Paul Cathcart is a Consultant Urological Surgeon at Guy's and St Thomas' NHS Foundation Trust, where he was appointed in 2015. He brings over fifteen years of consultant-level experience to his practice, having held a previous consultant appointment at University College London Hospitals (UCLH) from 2011.

He is one of the United Kingdom's most experienced robotic prostate surgeons, performing around 200 robotic prostatectomies each year. His particular expertise lies in nerve-sparing prostate cancer surgery, a technique that aims to preserve continence and sexual function while achieving optimal oncological outcomes.

Mr Cathcart also runs a specialist multidisciplinary team (MDT) clinic dedicated to the management of recurrent prostate cancer, including salvage robotic radical prostatectomy — a technically demanding procedure offered at only a small number of centres nationally.

Research & Publications

Advancing Urological Science

Mr Cathcart is the Chief Investigator for multiple clinical trials, including the RAFT study, which aims to improve outcomes for patients with prostate cancer. His commitment to evidence-based practice is reflected in a substantial body of published research.

He has authored more than sixteen peer-reviewed articles in leading international journals, contributing original research to the fields of uro-oncology, robotic surgery, and prostate cancer management.
