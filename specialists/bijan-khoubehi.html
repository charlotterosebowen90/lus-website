About Mr Khoubehi

Mr Bijan Khoubehi is a highly experienced Consultant Urological Surgeon with a distinguished career spanning more than two decades. He holds a senior NHS appointment at Chelsea and Westminster Hospital NHS Foundation Trust, where he serves as Lead Clinician for the urology department.

Recognised as a pioneer in robotic urological surgery, Mr Khoubehi has been performing robotic radical prostatectomies since 2007, making him one of the earliest adopters of this advanced technique in the United Kingdom. As a high-volume robotic surgeon, his published outcomes for cancer control, continence recovery and preservation of sexual function are comparable to the finest results reported internationally.

His expertise and reputation have earned recognition in Tatler's prestigious doctors' guide, and his work has been featured in national media including the Daily Mail and Mirror. Based in London, Mr Khoubehi is dedicated to delivering exceptional, patient-centred urological care.

Research & Publications

Mr Khoubehi has made significant contributions to urological science, with a particular emphasis on the molecular mechanisms underlying prostate cancer. His landmark research investigated the role of PAX2, a developmental gene, in prostate carcinogenesis — work that has advanced the understanding of how embryonic pathways become reactivated in malignancy.
