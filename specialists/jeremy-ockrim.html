About

A World Authority in Incontinence and Pelvic Reconstruction

Mr Jeremy Ockrim is an Associate Professor at University College London and a Consultant Urological Surgeon at University College London Hospitals (UCLH), where he leads the female and reconstructive urology service. Widely recognised as one of the foremost experts in incontinence and prolapse surgery, he was among the first surgeons in the United Kingdom to pioneer non-mesh surgical approaches for these conditions.

His clinical practice encompasses the full spectrum of female and male urinary incontinence, pelvic organ prolapse, mesh-related complications, urinary fistulae, bladder reconstruction, and complex functional bladder disorders. He brings over two decades of specialist experience to every patient consultation, combining technical precision with a compassionate, evidence-based approach to care.

Mr Ockrim is a member of the joint FFR-BSUG specialist unit for complex female incontinence, reflecting his standing as a trusted authority in the most challenging cases. Outside of clinical practice, he lives in London with his spouse and three children.

Research & Leadership

Driving Innovation Through Research

Mr Ockrim has authored more than 70 peer-reviewed publications spanning stress urinary incontinence, fistula repair, sacral neuromodulation, bladder reconstruction, and urethral diverticula. His research contributions continue to shape best practice in reconstructive and functional urology worldwide.

He currently serves as clinical lead for the MASTER study, a landmark multicentre trial investigating post-prostatectomy incontinence outcomes at UCLH. He also leads a comparative study evaluating sacral nerve neuromodulation against Botox therapy for refractory bladder dysfunction — work that promises to refine treatment algorithms for patients with complex voiding disorders.

In addition to his research leadership, Mr Ockrim holds the role of Assistant Editor at the Central European Journal of Urology, where he contributes to advancing the academic literature and peer-review standards in the field.
