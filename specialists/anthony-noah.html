About

About Mr Noah

Mr Anthony Noah is a Consultant Urological Surgeon practising at London Urology Specialists. He brings extensive clinical experience across the breadth of general urology, with particular expertise in adolescent urology, adult congenital urological conditions, and reconstructive surgery.

His practice is defined by a patient-centred approach that places equal emphasis on functional outcomes and quality of life. Whether managing complex congenital anomalies that persist into adulthood or addressing common urological concerns, Mr Noah is committed to providing individualised care tailored to each patient's unique circumstances.

Beyond his clinical work, Mr Noah is actively engaged in the education and mentorship of medical students and junior doctors, contributing to the development of the next generation of urological specialists.

Training & Education

Training & Background

Undergraduate Medical Training

Guy's, King's and St Thomas' School of Medicine

Mr Noah completed his undergraduate medical education at one of the UK's most established and prestigious medical schools, providing a robust foundation in clinical sciences and patient care.

Surgical Training

Birmingham and West Midlands

He undertook his core and higher surgical training across the Birmingham and West Midlands region, gaining extensive experience in both elective and emergency urological surgery.

Subspecialty Fellowship

University College London Hospitals (UCLH)

Mr Noah completed his subspecialty training at UCLH, one of the UK's leading tertiary referral centres for complex urology, where he developed his specialist interest in reconstructive and congenital urological surgery.

Philosophy

Clinical Approach

Patient-Centred, Outcome-Focused Care

Mr Noah believes that every patient deserves a bespoke clinical plan, carefully constructed around their individual needs, goals, and circumstances. His philosophy centres on achieving the best possible functional outcomes whilst preserving and enhancing quality of life.

This is particularly important in his work with patients who have congenital urological conditions, where long-term management requires continuity, sensitivity, and a deep understanding of how these conditions evolve over a lifetime. Mr Noah takes the time to explain all available options, ensuring patients feel informed and empowered to participate fully in decisions about their care.
