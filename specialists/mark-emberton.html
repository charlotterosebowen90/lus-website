About

A World-Leading Authority in Urological Oncology

Professor Mark Emberton is one of the most distinguished academic urologists practising in the United Kingdom. He holds the prestigious Chair of Interventional Oncology at University College London (UCL), where he leads ground-breaking research into the detection, surveillance, and minimally invasive treatment of prostate cancer.

As Director of the Division of Surgery and Interventional Science at UCL, Professor Emberton oversees a broad portfolio of translational research that bridges the gap between laboratory innovation and real-world patient care. His work has fundamentally shaped modern approaches to prostate cancer management, with a particular emphasis on precision diagnostics and tissue-preserving therapies.

Professor Emberton also serves as UCL Partners' Pathway Director for Urological Oncology within London Cancer, a role in which he coordinates cancer services for a population of approximately four million people across North London and beyond. This strategic leadership position reflects the breadth of his influence across both clinical practice and health-system design.

Pioneering Precision in Prostate Cancer Care

Professor Emberton's clinical practice is centred on the full spectrum of prostate cancer management — from early detection and active surveillance to cutting-edge, minimally invasive treatment. He directs a multidisciplinary innovation team that integrates bioengineering and nanotechnology with early-phase clinical trials, translating laboratory advances into patient-ready therapies.

Distinguished Standing in the Profession

Professor Emberton's clinical and academic excellence has earned widespread recognition. He has been named in Tatler's doctors' guide as one of the foremost urologists in the country — a distinction that reflects the esteem in which he is held by patients and peers alike.
