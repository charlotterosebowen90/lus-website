Your Journey

What to Expect

01

Before Your Appointment

Upon confirmation of your appointment, you will receive a detailed email with all the information you need ahead of your visit.

To support a seamless and highly focused consultation, we will ask you to provide a referral letter or a brief summary of your medical history and current symptoms.

It would also be helpful if you could provide a list of your current medications, along with any relevant test results, imaging, or medical reports from other specialists, prior to your appointment.

02

During Your Consultation

Your initial consultation typically lasts approximately 30 minutes. During this time, your consultant will take a comprehensive medical history, discuss your symptoms in detail, and, where clinically appropriate, carry out a physical examination.

Diagnostic tests or imaging may be arranged following your consultation, either on the same day where feasible or at a time that is convenient for you.

Your consultant will clearly explain their initial assessment and guide you through the most appropriate next steps, including any investigations or treatment options.

03

After Your Appointment

Following your consultation, you will receive a detailed letter summarising your assessment and recommended next steps. With your consent, this will also be shared with your GP and any other relevant healthcare professionals.

Our team will arrange any follow-up appointments, tests, or procedures as discussed with your consultant.

If you are self-funding, our team will provide a written quotation for any recommended tests, ensuring you are fully informed before you choose to proceed.

04

Procedure Arrangements

If a procedure is recommended, your consultant will discuss the available options with you and agree a clear treatment plan before any arrangements are made.

Once you have chosen to proceed, the booking process will be managed by the consultant's secretary, who will coordinate with you, the hospital and the anaesthetist to secure a suitable date. You will receive an admission letter confirming the procedural details, including location and expected length of stay; this also serves as a written quotation for the consultant's surgical fee.

For self-funding patients, a separate written quotation will be provided by the hospital outlining their associated fees, ensuring full transparency before proceeding.

Any pre-assessment requirements will be arranged directly by the hospital team, who will contact you in advance of your procedure.

Should you have any questions at any stage, our team remains available to support you.

Insurance & Payment

Private Medical Insurance

We are recognised by all major private medical insurers.

Before your appointment, please contact your insurer to obtain authorisation. Once confirmed, email your policy number and authorisation code to your consultant's secretary or:
accounts@londonurologyspecialists.co.uk

Please also bring these details with you on the day of your appointment.

Embassy Referrals

We welcome patients referred through international embassies and government-sponsored healthcare programmes.

Our team has extensive experience working with embassy referral pathways and can coordinate directly with your sponsoring body to ensure a smooth process from booking through to treatment.

Please contact us directly so we can guide you through the next steps.

Frequently Asked Questions

A GP referral is not required for private consultations, although we recommend having one as it provides valuable background on your medical history.

If you are using private medical insurance, your insurer may require a referral letter for authorisation.

We aim to offer appointments within 24–48 hours of your enquiry. Where clinically appropriate, same-day appointments may also be available.

Please contact our team to check current availability.

  • New patient consultations will not exceed £340
  • Follow-up appointments will not exceed £230

If you are using private medical insurance, please check with your insurer to confirm your cover and any authorisation requirements.

Please bring:

  • A list of your current medications
  • Any relevant test results or imaging (e.g. scans, blood tests)
  • Your referral letter, if you have one
  • Any medical reports from other specialists (if applicable)
  • Your insurance details (if applicable)

We recommend arriving 15 minutes early to allow time for registration.

Where appropriate, simple tests such as blood or urine tests may be arranged on the same day as your consultation.

Imaging and procedures are typically scheduled for a separate appointment, allowing for appropriate planning and giving you the flexibility to choose a convenient date and time.
